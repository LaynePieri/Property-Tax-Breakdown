Sources & Methodology

This page lists the public documents and assumptions used to build the property tax breakdown calculator. It exists so residents can verify the data, understand the limitations, or reuse this model for other communities.

Primary sources

  • FY26 Adopted Budget Summary and Supporting Schedules (City of Marshalltown)
  • City Council Proceedings – April 28, 2025 (FY26 Budget Adoption)

These documents are publicly available through the City of Marshalltown’s records and City Council packet archives.

What is included

This calculator includes Governmental Activities with non‑zero FY26 expenditures, using the standard functional categories required under Iowa municipal budget reporting.

  • Public Safety
  • Public Works
  • Health & Social Services
  • Culture & Recreation
  • Community & Economic Development
  • General Government
  • Debt Service
  • Capital Projects

Each category represents many services and activities grouped together, not a single department or project.

What is not included

  • Utility or enterprise funds (such as water, sewer, or electric)
  • Individual departments, programs, or line items
  • Grant funding or special revenue restrictions
  • Budget amendments adopted after FY26 approval
  • Individual property characteristics or tax situations

These exclusions are intentional and help keep the calculator readable, comparable, and resident‑focused.

Note on the “Permanent” category

Iowa budget law requires cities to report spending in nine functional categories, including Permanent, which is used for certain trust or endowment funds whose principal is generally not spent.

In Marshalltown’s FY26 adopted budget, the Permanent category reports $0 in expenditures. Because this calculator distributes only actual spending, the Permanent category is noted for completeness but excluded from the calculations.

Methodology (plain language)

  1. Total FY26 Governmental Activities spending was taken from the adopted budget
  2. Each category’s share of that total was calculated as a percentage
  3. Those percentages are applied to the amount a user enters

Results are shown in whole dollars. Small rounding differences are handled by adjusting the final category so totals always match the entered amount.

No assumptions are made about how individual services or projects were funded.

Planned future expansion

Future versions of this site may include optional, clearly labeled examples of individual budget line items within categories—such as the smallest, largest, or most typical expenditures—to show the range of services packed into each category.

Any such additions will be separately sourced and presented as illustrative context, not exhaustive listings.
