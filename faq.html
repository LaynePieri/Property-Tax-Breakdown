Frequently Asked Questions

This page answers common questions people ask when using this site. If you are wondering whether something is missing, biased, or confusing, you will likely find the answer here.

Is this an official City of Marshalltown website?

No. This site is an independent project created by a Marshalltown resident. It is not affiliated with the City or any government office.

Are you arguing for higher or lower taxes?

No. The site does not recommend tax changes or budget decisions. It only shows how existing property tax structures work and how City services are funded in practice.

Why doesn’t this match my property tax bill exactly?

Property tax bills include multiple governments and individual factors such as credits, exemptions, and assessments. This tool focuses only on the City share and uses citywide averages.

Why is my City share smaller than I expected?

Most property tax payments go to schools, counties, and other local entities. The City receives only a portion of the total bill.

Why does my school district affect the City’s share?

The City tax rate is the same throughout Marshalltown. School districts have different tax rates, which change the total bill and therefore the percentage that goes to the City.

Are these numbers exact?

The tax rates and budget totals come from official, certified documents. Individual results are estimates because each property is different.

Why aren’t utilities or grants included?

Utilities are funded by user fees, not property taxes. Grants often have legal restrictions that do not apply citywide. To keep things readable, those revenues are excluded.

What am I supposed to conclude from this?

There is no required conclusion. The purpose of this site is understanding, not persuasion. You may agree or disagree with current policies after seeing the data.

What if something looks wrong?

Corrections and good‑faith feedback are welcome. If a number or explanation appears incorrect, it should be fixed rather than defended.
