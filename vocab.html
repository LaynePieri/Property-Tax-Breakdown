Vocabulary

This page explains words and phrases used on this site in plain language. If you are unsure what a term means, check here before assuming it is complicated.

Property tax

Money paid by property owners to local governments each year. Property tax bills are usually shared across several governments, not just the City.

Levy (tax rate)

The amount of tax charged per $1,000 of taxable property value. Different governments set different levy rates.

Total tax rate

The combined levy from all local governments on a property, including the City, school district, county, and others.

City share

The portion of a total property tax bill that goes to the City after all levies are added together.

School district

The public school system a property is assigned to. School district tax rates vary and affect how a tax bill is divided.

Governmental activities

Core services a city provides to the public, such as police, streets, parks, and administration. These are usually funded by taxes rather than user fees.

Enterprise or utility funds

City operations that charge users directly for services, such as water, sewer, or electric utilities. These are not included in this calculator.

Functional categories

Standard groupings used in city budgets to organize services. Each category includes many related activities.

Public Safety

Services that protect people and property, such as police and fire.

Public Works

Services that maintain physical infrastructure, including streets, sidewalks, and stormwater systems.

General Government

Administrative support that allows city services to operate, such as finance, legal, and management functions.

Debt service

Payments the City makes on borrowed money, including principal and interest.

Capital projects

Large, long‑term investments like buildings, major equipment, or infrastructure improvements.

Certified

Officially approved and finalized for use, usually by a county or state authority.

Adopted budget

A city’s final spending plan for the year, approved by the City Council through a formal vote.
