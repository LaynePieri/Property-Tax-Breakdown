What this estimate represents

This tool does not trace individual tax dollars to specific services. City revenues are pooled and legally restricted, so direct attribution does not exist in the adopted budget.

Instead, your property tax payment is used as a reference amount and applied proportionally across spending categories based on their share of total Governmental Activities spending.

Amounts are shown in whole dollars. Small rounding differences are handled by adjusting the final category so totals always match the amount entered.

What the City delivered in FY26

Below is a snapshot of what major service areas cost the City as a whole. This provides context for understanding the scale of services residents receive citywide, regardless of funding source.

Public Safety

Citywide cost: ~$13.3 million
Includes police, fire, emergency response, inspections, and related safety services.

Public Works

Citywide cost: ~$11.9 million
Includes streets, sidewalks, stormwater systems, snow removal, and infrastructure.

Health & Social Services

Citywide cost: ~$2.0 million
Public and environmental health functions and limited social service programs.

Culture & Recreation

Citywide cost: ~$3.9 million
Parks, recreation programs, library services, and cultural amenities.

Community & Economic Development

Citywide cost: ~$3.9 million
Planning, housing-related activities, and economic development efforts.

General Government

Citywide cost: ~$2.2 million
Governance, finance, legal services, elections, and administration.

Debt Service

Citywide cost: ~$6.7 million
Principal and interest payments on existing city debt.

Capital Projects

Citywide cost: ~$15.9 million
Construction, major repairs, and long-term investments in public facilities.

Figures are rounded and shown for context. Categories group many services and are not exhaustive.
