Where Your City Property Taxes Go (FY26)

What this is

This is a non‑official, resident‑focused tool designed to help people understand—at a high level—how property tax dollars support city services in Marshalltown. It translates the City of Marshalltown’s FY26 adopted budget into plain language and shows an estimated breakdown based on how much you paid in property taxes.

What this is not

This is not an official City of Marshalltown website or calculator. It does not replace official budget documents, tax statements, or financial reports. It does not reflect fund restrictions, grant funding, timing differences, or individual property circumstances. This tool exists to improve understanding, not to make financial or legal determinations.

Where the data comes from

All figures are based on the City of Marshalltown’s FY26 adopted budget, which was approved by City Council on April 28, 2025. Only Governmental Activities with actual FY26 expenditures are included. Utilities and other enterprise funds are excluded.

Privacy

No personal or financial information is collected, stored, or shared. All calculations are performed in real time using the number you enter. Enter your estimate Estimated annual property tax paid ($) You can enter the amount from your property tax statement, or use a rough estimate. Want a more accurate estimate? You can look up your property’s assessed value through the Marshall County Assessor. Property values shown there may reflect earlier valuation dates, but they are the best public source for estimating taxes. How property value becomes property tax (simplified): The county assessor determines a property’s taxable value. Cities, counties, schools, and other local governments set tax rates. Your property tax is calculated by multiplying the taxable value by the combined tax rates (usually shown as dollars per $1,000 of value). If you know how much you actually paid, entering that amount will give the most useful estimate here. Your estimated breakdown (FY26) The amounts below show how your estimated property tax payment would be proportionally allocated across major city service areas, based on FY26 budgeted spending. These are estimates. Amounts are shown in whole dollars and adjusted so the total matches what you entered. Public Safety Supports police, fire, emergency response, inspections, and other services focused on protecting people, property, and public safety. Public Works Covers streets, sidewalks, traffic infrastructure, stormwater systems, snow removal, and the maintenance of core public infrastructure. Health & Social Services Includes programs and services that support public health, environmental health, and community social services. Culture & Recreation Supports parks, recreation programs, library services, cultural amenities, and community spaces used by the public. Community & Economic Development Includes planning, housing‑related activities, economic development efforts, and programs intended to support neighborhood and community growth. General Government Covers administrative functions needed to operate the city, such as governance, finance, legal services, elections, and basic city operations. Debt Service Represents payments on existing city debt, including principal and interest for past capital investments. Capital Projects Includes construction, major repairs, and long‑term investments in public facilities and infrastructure. Each category groups many services together. Descriptions are illustrative and not exhaustive. Note on the “Permanent” category Iowa budget law requires cities to report spending in nine functional categories, including Permanent, which is used for certain trust or endowment funds. In Marshalltown’s FY26 adopted budget, the Permanent category reports no expenditures, so it is noted here but not included in the calculations. How the math works (plain language) This tool: Uses total FY26 Governmental Activities spending from the adopted budget. Calculates the percentage share of each category. Applies those percentages to the number you enter. No assumptions are made about specific funding sources for individual services or projects. Final, optional footer line (recommended) This calculator reflects Governmental Activities with non‑zero expenditures in the FY26 adopted City of Marshalltown budget.
